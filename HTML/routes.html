Routes

Here you will find some of the most popular running spots in Philadelphia. Jump in with the vast community of runners and scroll through the several places they run at.

Schuylkill River Trail: Banks and Boardwalk

From South Street Bridge, taking the ramp down onto the boardwalk. Continuing along the path to behind the Philadelphia Art Museum closing on 1.6 miles.

Boxer's Trail

Home to the once-training grounds of famous boxers, Muhammed Ali and Joe Frazier. It will certainly provide the fighting spirit you need for a great run!

Benjamin Franklin Bridge Pedestrian Walkway

The walkway is a 1.5-mile-long pedestrian and bike path that runs along the bridge and offers stunning views of the Delaware River and the Philadelphia skyline.

Bartram's Garden

A 45-acre public park along the Schuylkill River that features gardens, trails, and meadows. Runners can enjoy several paved and unpaved trails that wind through the park's meadows and gardens, providing a peaceful and scenic escape from the city.

FDR Park

The park offers several trails that wind through wooded areas and provide stunning views of the Philadelphia skyline, making it an ideal place to find respite from the city and appreciate the beauty of the natural world.

Kelly Drive

The Kelly Drive is one of Philly's most popular running and biking destinations. It is a nearly 9-mile lap that hugs the Schuylkill River. The lovely scenery of Kelly Drive; dependent on the season include weeping cherry trees with beautiful pink blooms

Belmont Plateau

The park offers several trails that wind through wooded areas and provide stunning views of the Philadelphia skyline, making it an ideal place to retreat from the urban landscape and immerse oneself in nature.
